We're excited to announce the launch of **PySimHub**, a community hub for Python simulation and numerics tools!

## Our Mission

The Python ecosystem has an incredible wealth of simulation libraries spanning robotics, fluid dynamics, discrete-event simulation, agent-based modeling, and many more domains. However, discovering these tools can be challengingâ€”they're often siloed within specific communities and hard to find unless you already know where to look.

PySimHub aims to solve this by creating a **unified discovery platform** where researchers, engineers, and developers can explore simulation tools across all domains.

## What We Cover

Our catalog spans multiple simulation domains:

- **Robotics** - Robot simulation, kinematics, and motion planning
- **Fluid Dynamics** - CFD, flow simulation, and turbulence modeling
- **Discrete-Event** - Process simulation and queueing systems
- **Agent-Based** - Multi-agent systems and emergent behavior
- **System Dynamics** - Feedback loops and block diagram models
- **FEM/PDE** - Finite element methods and PDEs
- **Molecular Dynamics** - Molecular simulation and chemistry
- **Control Systems** - Control theory and automation

## Open from Day One

PySimHub is **open to submissions from day one**. If you've built or maintain a Python simulation library, we want to hear from you! Submit your project through our [GitHub Issue template](/submit) and join our growing catalog.

## What's Next

We have exciting plans for PySimHub:

1. **Growing the catalog** - We're actively seeking submissions from the community
2. **Automated updates** - Soon, project statistics will update automatically from GitHub
3. **Community features** - Blog posts, tutorials, and eventually a small conference

## Get Involved

There are several ways to contribute to PySimHub:

- **Submit a project** - [Add your simulation library](/submit) to our catalog
- **Spread the word** - Share PySimHub with your colleagues and communities
- **Contribute** - Help improve the website on [GitHub](https://github.com/pysimhub)

Thank you for being part of the Python simulation community. We're excited to build this together!
